Most importantly: All our food preservation products are based on natural sources - which means they can be claimed as natural preservatives.

Our packaging solution, Pack-Age™ is a great example of our combined knowledge and expertise in Life Sciences and Materials Science.

This recent innovation enables cheese producers to efficiently control and balance their cheese ripening process. Because Pack-Age™ film is perm-selective, it supplies just enough moisture to the cheese while maintaining an effective oxygen barrier against mold growth on the cheese's surface.

When applied in combination with natamycin, this new way of ripening provides superior protection against yeast and mold growth.

The result: a naturally ripened cheese with a matured taste and firm texture – with a significant yield improvement and substantially improved carbon footprint over traditionally coated cheese.

Reducing global food waste

In 2011, the UN's Food and Agriculture Organization reported that: "Roughly one third of the food produced in the world for human consumption – approximately 1.3 billion tons - gets lost or wasted". That’s a staggering amount considering the global distribution of food and population growth rates.

In developing countries, the problem is not human wastage but rather that 30-50% of all food is lost in production by spoilage and through poor packaging. In the West, there’s a growing demand for smaller portion sizes and individual packets to meet changing demographic and smaller households.

Reducing global food waste

Bottom line: You can extend shelf life and preserve the food's natural vitality. That’s good for people, planet - and profit.

We're a global leader in value-added ingredient solutions for cheese production, preservation and protection.

Cheese production is of course a fine art and the production process has several steps, from the transition of milk towards the cheese product, followed by the ripening process. This process can take a few weeks up to several years.

One of the most critical factors in cheese production and ripening is to avoid the growth of unwanted microbial growth. These micro-organisms can be introduced by the milk from the farm and during the production and ripening process.

DSM developed solutions to tackle the problem at every one of these very different steps. In addition to differences in the general manufacturing process, each cheese also has its own specific characteristics that can be optimized through the select use of preservatives.

Then of course there's our innovative packaging film, Pack-Age™ - which offers different benefits for different cheese types. In some cases it can enhance yield by preventing too much loss of moisture; it can reduce waste and improve quality thanks to more consistent drying; and it can even improves taste and texture in comparison to barrier films. Overall, compared to regular coated cheese, it substantially reduces the carbon footprint per kg sliced cheese.

Cheese production starts of course with milk. In this stage DELVO®ZYME can be added to control unwanted growth of Clostridia bacteria and so prevent late blowing defects (big eye-formation and off taste) in certain cheese types.

Curd

During curd production you must be beware of microbial spoilage. You can treat equipment with DELVO®CID to control yeast and mold.

Brine bath

Some cheese types are salted by a brining process that can last a few hours up to more than 5 days. DELVO®CID+ offers optimal brine and yeast control and extra security in controlling the microbial quality of your brine.

Spray/dip bath

Upon leaving the brine bath, cheeses are unprotected and vulnerable to yeast and mold. You can spray or dip consisting of DELVO®CID+ or DELVO®COAT L to protect them.

During ripening, cheeses are exposed to mold and yeast spoilage. We have several solutions for traditional ripening, film ripening, our innovative Pack-Age™ ripening and our DELVO®COAT customized coatings.

Add an anti-cake powder or spray solution to ensure shredded cheese is safe and protected from your factory to the consumer. Add DELVO®CID and/or DELVO®NIS to protect spreadable cheeses from bacteria, yeast and mold.

Proper manufacturing use of our solutions during production enables longer shelf life and fresher products at the customer end, In addition, products can be packed in a gas-atmosphere or pasteurized and packed with sealing.
